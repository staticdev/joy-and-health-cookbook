# Pickled Onions

## Ingredients

- 3 standard mason jars (400ml each)
- 2 medium red onions (about 300–400g)
- vinegar
    - 1 cup rice vinager: light, sweet and fruity
    - 1 cup apple cider vinager: stronger, very fruity
    - ¾ cup distilled white vinager: very strong, clean taste
- 2 cups water
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 2 tsp salt
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 2 bay leaves
- Optional: chilly flakes

## Tip

Do not cry!

Use pair of safety glasses/goggles that are dustproof/sealed to cut onions. Eg.:

## Instructions

1. Prepare the onions
    1. Peel the skin, cut the onions in halves.
    1. Chop the red onions around the onion shape:
        1. For crunchy: more thich slices.
        1. For not crunchy: thin slices.
    1. Put the onions to the side into a clean heatproof jar or container.
1. Make the brine
    1. Get a measuring cup for 3 cups.
    1. Combine the vinegar and water in the measuring cup.
    1. Put it in a pan, add oregano, sugar and salt.
1. Put the cinnamon sticks, and bay leaves inside the jar with the onions. Optional chilly flakes.
1. Heat the brine to simmering (not boiling) and stir until the sugar and salt are fully dissolved.
1. Carefully pour the hot brine over the sliced onions so they are fully submerged. Press down to remove air pockets if needed.
1. Close the lid and shake the jar upside down.
1. Let the jar cool to room temperature, then put to refrigerate. Let the onions there overnight.

## Storage and serving

1. Store refrigerated for up to 2–3 weeks.
1. Use as a topping for tacos, salads, sandwiches, or grilled meats.
